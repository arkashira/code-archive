# marketing-plan.md

## Positioning
**Primary Positioning Statement:**  
"Code Archive: The ultimate backup and restore solution for developers, seamlessly integrated with your favorite tools."

**Alternative Positioning Statements:**  
1. "Never lose your code again with Code Archive – your reliable backup partner."
2. "Code Archive: Effortless data protection for developers, ensuring peace of mind."
3. "Transform your development workflow with Code Archive – smart, secure, and simple backups."

## Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)
1. **Persona 1: Alex the Freelance Developer**  
   - **Daily Job:** Works on multiple client projects, often switching between different codebases.  
   - **Budget:** $50/month for tools and services.  

2. **Persona 2: Jamie the Startup CTO**  
   - **Daily Job:** Oversees a small team of developers, focusing on product development and deployment.  
   - **Budget:** $500/month for development tools and infrastructure.  

3. **Persona 3: Sam the DevOps Engineer**  
   - **Daily Job:** Manages CI/CD pipelines and ensures data integrity across environments.  
   - **Budget:** $200/month for operational tools and services.  

## Channels
1. **Developer Forums and Communities (e.g., Reddit, Stack Overflow)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $20 per acquisition through targeted ads and community engagement.  

2. **Content Marketing (Blogs, Tutorials, Webinars)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $15 per acquisition through organic traffic and lead generation.  

3. **Partnerships with Development Tool Providers (e.g., GitHub, GitLab)**  
   - **CAC Estimate:** $30 per acquisition through co-marketing efforts and integrations.  

## Content Cadence
- **Week 1:** Launch blog post introducing Code Archive; share on social media.  
- **Week 2:** Create tutorial video on setting up Code Archive with popular IDEs.  
- **Week 3:** Host a webinar on best practices for backup and restore in development.  
- **Week 4:** Publish a case study featuring early adopters of Code Archive.  
- **Week 5:** Share user testimonials and feedback on social media.  
- **Week 6:** Release an infographic on the importance of data backup for developers.  
- **Week 7:** Write a guest post on a popular developer blog about backup strategies.  
- **Week 8:** Conduct a Q&A session on social media to engage with potential users.  
- **Week 9:** Launch a limited-time promotional offer for early adopters.  
- **Week 10:** Share insights and analytics from user engagement to refine messaging.  
- **Week 11:** Release a follow-up blog post on new features based on user feedback.  
- **Week 12:** Prepare a launch recap and future roadmap announcement.  

## Launch Milestones
- **D-30:** Finalize product features and begin beta testing with selected users.  
- **D-0:** Official product launch; initiate marketing campaigns across all channels.  
- **D+30:** Gather user feedback, implement necessary adjustments, and release updates.  
- **D+90:** Evaluate product performance, user engagement, and prepare for next phase of growth.  

## Success Metrics
- **Daily Active Users (DAU):** Target 1,000 DAU by D+90.  
- **Monthly Recurring Revenue ($MRR):** Target $10,000 MRR by D+90.
